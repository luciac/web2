Jacket

The best way to protect your arms is with a good solid jacket. A strong enough jacket will stop most zombie bites. They won’t stop a machete if you do something stupid accidentally, but they will protect from bites and scratches. The best materials for jackets are real leather, and canvas. Because your arms extend so far from the rest of the body they are quite vulnerable.

Best Materials

  • Leather

    Leather is the best option for colder weather. Its stronger than nearly all man made textiles and is extremely tear and puncture resistant. They also give you extra bad-ass points.

  • Canvas

    Canvas, although man made, is very durable and usually used for heavy outdoor work. Just try biting your way through a canvas jacket and you'll understand.

  • Duck

    Duck fabric is like canvas, only stronger. Which basically just makes it even better.

Boots

Feet are your main vehicle. They need to be protected. Boots not only protect you from zombies, they also protect you from the elements. Waterproof boots keep your feet dry which is much more important than you could possibly realize. Wet boots are heavier, which in turn will tire you out more. Wet feet for a long period of time can lead to sickness. The last thing you want is a terrible cold when you’re trying to run for your life. Good boots will help you through snow as well.

The right boot to buy is a good strong athletic pair that covers your ankles. Hiking boots work best. But make sure to break them in and wear good socks. Blisters could mean the end of you. Athletic boots were designed and built for long usage in rough terrain, which is why they are your best option.

Machete

Now that you have a long range weapon you’ll need a close range one. If a zombie is all up in your face the crossbow won’t be as easy to use. So now is the time for melee weapons. Machetes are one of the best options for close range combat.

The machete is a long and very sharp blade. Its orginal use is for cutting undergrowth in the rain forests, which can consist of strong, thick vines. Some people even use the machete to cut open coconuts. This is a great sign because coconuts are generally tougher than the human skull. If something can damage a coconut, it can damage a human skull easily. If you want to practice using your machete, using a coconut may be the way to go.

Tent

No matter where you are, you will need to sleep. More importantly, you need to sleep where zombies can’t get you. If you can find a safe and fortified place, go for it. If you can’t, then the tensile tent is your perfect solution. Tensile tents are tents that hang from trees. There are plenty of trees in most cities: along streets, in parks, on campus grounds. Making sure they look thick enough is important.

There are two main types. Ones that hang for one point at the top, and ones that latch on from two or more points on the sides. The brand Tensile hangs from the sides meaning you’ll need two trees decently spaced apart. Although that’s an obvious downside, they are typically easier to set up than the ones that hang from one point. Time, in life and death situtaions, is paramount. The tensile tent also comes with the option of two person tents and three person tents.

Food

Next you need to worry about getting nutrients. In order to keep going you'll need quick food, with lots of nutrients and calories.

  • Beef Jerky

    Beef jerky is one of the best apocalyptic food options available. It can last from three months if homemade to two years store bought and unopened. The longevity of the food is it’s main appeal. However it’s nutritional value is extremely helpful. Beef jerky is filled with protein. Protein helps build and repair tissue. Protein is a “macronutrient” which means the body needs large amounts of. Picking food with high levels of fat, carbohydrates, and protein is important.

  • Dried Fruit

    They are nutritious and can last from six months to a full year. They are packed with calories and high in carbohydrates. This makes dry fruit a great source of quick energy

  • Granola

    Granola bars and cereal bars have a mix of nuts, seeds, oils, cereal, granola and sometimes fruit making them an excellent mix of nutrients.
